## Source and terminology policy

Use sources in this order:

1. Nintendo's direct Simplified Chinese pages and exact in-game Simplified Chinese localization.
2. The bundled glossary for established local consistency and documented aliases.
3. Nintendo's Japanese or English material for meaning and context.
4. Nintendo's other official Chinese regions when no Simplified Chinese form exists.
5. Broadly established mainland Simplified Chinese community usage for readability.

For a current season, event, update, newly announced game, or term not in the glossary, verify online when tools permit. Prefer Nintendo game pages, news, support pages, videos, and in-game captures. Use community wikis or discussions only to establish community usage, not to claim official status. Record the URL and whether the conclusion is official, community-established, or inferred.

If official sources conflict, resolve by product, region, date, and usage context. Do not assume one source globally overrides another.

When sources conflict:

- Use the term requested by the user.
- Otherwise use the established mainland community term in the translation.
- Add the official regional name or competing common alias to `关键术语`.
- If context changes the meaning, do not force one global replacement.

Never infer that an older fan translation is the current official name. Do not invent a Chinese abbreviation by combining known abbreviations. Treat all future or recently announced Splatoon titles as time-sensitive.

## Scene-specific style

### UI and system text

Use compact verb-object labels. Preserve variables, controller buttons, counts, units, and menu hierarchy. Prefer functional clarity over reproducing English title case or Japanese nominalization.

### Announcements and patch notes

Preserve dates, time zones, eligibility, exceptions, probability language, and version numbers exactly. Convert relative dates such as “tomorrow” only when the source date and target time zone are known; otherwise retain the relative expression and note the ambiguity.

### Dialogue

Infer the speaker only from evidence. Preserve excitement, hesitation, repeated sounds, catchphrases, and register. Recreate puns when a compact Chinese equivalent exists; otherwise translate the immediate meaning and add one short note.

### Guides and community posts

Use familiar player phrasing, but do not add strategy advice or certainty absent from the source. On first occurrence, prefer `首选术语（常见别名）` only when the alias materially helps the audience; use the chosen form thereafter.

### Names and fictional branding

Check whether a string is a character, band, brand, stage, weapon kit, song, award, event, or ordinary noun before translating it.

For an unknown proper noun, preserve the source spelling in Chinese corner brackets: `「原文」`. Do not create a phonetic transliteration.

- Use `《》` only for confirmed titles of games, books, comics, songs, and other works.
- Use `“”` for quoted speech, quoted wording, or ordinary emphasis.
- Use `「」` as the editorial marker for an untranslated person, place, group, item, or fictional proper noun.
- Do not use `【】`; it normally functions as a heading, category, or interface-style label rather than a proper-name marker.

## Difficult language

- **Katakana coinages:** determine whether the word is a pun, brand, sound effect, species reference, or borrowed English. Translate its function when reliable; otherwise preserve it as `「原文」`.
- **English ink/squid puns:** prioritize the sentence's function and tone. Explain only wordplay that affects interpretation.
- **Onomatopoeia:** render its gameplay or emotional effect naturally rather than mapping syllables mechanically.
- **Splat:** translate by function. It can mean defeating a player, being defeated, an ink impact, or franchise branding.
- **Fresh:** distinguish approval/rank flavor from literal freshness.
- **Player slang:** identify the underlying weapon, tactic, route, role, or rank concept. Keep a common Chinese nickname when it is unambiguous; otherwise include the formal term.
- **Japanese omission:** restore subjects only when context establishes them. Avoid assigning a line to a character based on stereotypes.
- **Named awards and event labels:** do not turn a literal interpretation into an official-looking Chinese title. If no verified Chinese name exists, keep `「原文」` and explain its likely meaning only when useful.

## Screenshots

1. Inspect the whole image before reading individual text regions.
2. Transcribe from panels and visual groups, not from raw OCR order.
3. Use icons, colors, counters, and layout only as contextual evidence.
4. Preserve separate text blocks in the translation.
5. Mark uncertain characters explicitly. Do not repair a weapon or stage name solely because it resembles a known term.
6. If the screenshot is cropped, mention missing context only when it affects the translation.

## Review procedure

For translation review:

1. Compare source and translation segment by segment.
2. Classify issues as meaning, terminology, omission, tone, fluency, formatting, or unverifiable claim.
3. Explain only actionable issues.
4. Produce a complete clean revision after the issue list.
5. Check repeated terms globally and keep one chosen form unless context requires otherwise.

For an unknown term, report:

- source spelling and language;
- likely category and game/version;
- chosen rendering;
- official or community evidence;
- confidence: high, medium, or low.
